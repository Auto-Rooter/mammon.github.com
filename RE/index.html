R.E. HQ

the science of reverse engineering


Reverse engineering is, as the name implies, a reversal of the engineering process. A prefabricated product or component is taken apart and studied until the materials and the methods of its construction are understood.

The goal of reverse engineering is often to replicate the engineering process, so that an expensive or contraband product can be produced cheaply and locally. In some applications, only information about a key process or material is required in order to supplement an existing engineering effort. Finally, the goal might be to uncover weaknesses or flaws with which to counter the engineered product -- for example, one might wish to find flaws in an encryption algorithm, or to recognize signatures in a viral infector, or to determine the frequency of a type of transmitter.

The question has often been posed, "Is reverse engineering possible?". The answer in any case is yes; that which has been made, can be unmade. Whether it is cost- or time-efficient to do so, however, is another matter entirely.


This site is primarily concerned with reverse engineering ["RE"] in the computer sciences, although other disciplines may be touched upon where they help to advance reverse engineering processes and techniques.
